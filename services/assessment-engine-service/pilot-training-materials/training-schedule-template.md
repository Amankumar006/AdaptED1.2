# Training Schedule Template - Assessment Engine Pilot

## Program Overview

**Program Duration**: 2 weeks (10 business days)  
**Pilot Duration**: 30 days following training  
**Participants**: 3 pilot teachers, 50 students across 3 cohorts  
**Training Format**: Hybrid (in-person and virtual sessions)

## Pre-Training Preparation (Week Before Training)

### Administrative Tasks
- [ ] **Participant Confirmation** (Day -7)
  - Confirm teacher participation
  - Verify student cohort assignments
  - Send calendar invitations for all sessions
  - Distribute pre-training materials

- [ ] **Technical Setup** (Day -5)
  - Create pilot user accounts
  - Configure pilot environment
  - Test all systems and integrations
  - Prepare training materials and resources

- [ ] **Resource Distribution** (Day -3)
  - Send teacher quick start guide
  - Distribute student onboarding materials
  - Share technical requirements checklist
  - Provide support contact information

## Contingency Plans

### Technical Issues
- **Backup Systems**: Alternative access methods prepared
- **Extended Support**: Additional technical support on standby
- **Delayed Launch**: Criteria and process for launch delay
- **Rollback Plan**: Procedure to revert to previous system if needed

### Participation Issues
- **Substitute Teachers**: Backup teachers identified and trained
- **Student Absences**: Make-up training sessions planned
- **Low Engagement**: Additional motivation and support strategies
- **Resistance Management**: Change management and communication plans

### Schedule Conflicts
- **Flexible Scheduling**: Alternative session times available
- **Recorded Sessions**: Key sessions recorded for later viewing
- **Individual Support**: One-on-one sessions for missed training
- **Accelerated Training**: Condensed training for late joiners

This comprehensive training schedule ensures all participants are well-prepared for the pilot program and have the support they need for success. The schedule can be customized based on specific institutional needs and constraints.
